# Forecasting Overview

This guide provides an overview of the forecasting capabilities available in the platform and explains how finance teams can create, manage, and refine forecasts at scale.

Forecasting is designed for enterprise planning scenarios where accuracy, auditability, and collaboration are critical.

---

## What you can do with Forecasting

Using Forecasting, you can:

- Create rolling and static forecasts across time horizons
- Model multiple forecast scenarios for comparison
- Incorporate historical actuals and assumptions
- Collaborate across finance and business teams
- Leverage AI-assisted insights to identify trends and anomalies

---

## Key forecasting concepts

### Forecast versions
Forecasts are created as versions, allowing you to:
- Maintain a baseline forecast
- Create alternative scenarios (Best Case, Worst Case)
- Lock versions for audit and reporting purposes

### Driver-based forecasting
Driver-based models allow forecast values to be calculated using:
- Growth rates
- Headcount drivers
- Volume-based assumptions

This approach improves consistency and reduces manual effort.

---

## AI-assisted forecasting insights

AI-assisted forecasting analyzes historical data and forecast inputs to:
- Highlight unusual variances
- Surface potential risks early
- Suggest adjustment areas based on past patterns

These insights are advisory and do not modify forecast values automatically.

---

## Governance and controls

Forecasting includes enterprise-grade controls such as:
- Role-based access
- Approval workflows
- Change history tracking
- Forecast locking after submission

---

## When to use Forecasting

Forecasting is typically used:
- During monthly or quarterly planning cycles
- For scenario modeling and sensitivity analysis
- To support executive reporting and decision-making
